- [ ] Valid passport (6+ months validity beyond return date)

- [ ] Travel insurance certificate (keep digital + printed copy)

### Clothing and Luggage
- Check weather for all destinations during your travel dates
- For beach destinations: reef-safe sunscreen is required in many marine parks
- For temple visits: bring a lightweight scarf or sarong to cover shoulders/knees
- Most airlines allow 1 carry-on (7–10kg) and 1 checked bag (20–23kg) in economy
- SunPath packages include baggage fees for one checked bag per traveler

### Electronics
- Universal power adapter (especially needed for Europe, UK, Asia)
- Portable battery bank (phones die fast with GPS and maps running)
- Download offline maps (Google Maps, Maps.me) before leaving
- Consider a local SIM card on arrival — we can pre-arrange for major destinations

## Health and Safety

### Before You Travel
- Visit your GP or travel clinic at least 8 weeks before departure
- Check required and recommended vaccinations for your destination
- Ensure you have enough prescription medications for the entire trip + 7 extra days
- Get dental and eye checkups before long trips — treatment abroad can be expensive

### Common Health Precautions
**Tropical destinations (Thailand, Bali, Vietnam, India)**:
- Use DEET-based mosquito repellent (dengue, malaria risk in some areas)
- Drink only bottled or filtered water
- Eat freshly cooked food, avoid raw salads at street stalls
- Apply sunscreen SPF 50+ daily

**Europe and developed destinations**:
- Tap water is generally safe to drink
- Standard food safety applies
- Pharmacies are widely available

## Money and Finances

### Before You Leave
- Notify your bank of travel dates and destinations to prevent card blocks
- Get some local currency before departure (airport rates are expensive — use city ATMs)
- Consider a travel card with no foreign transaction fees (Wise, Revolut, or Charles Schwab)
- Keep $200 USD or €150 as emergency cash in a separate place from your wallet

### At Your Destination
- Use ATMs affiliated with major banks for better exchange rates
- Avoid currency exchange booths in tourist areas (high commission)
- Always pay in local currency when given the option (avoids dynamic currency conversion fees)
- Keep receipts for large purchases (needed for VAT refunds in Europe)

### VAT Refunds in Europe
EU tourists can claim VAT refunds on purchases over €50–€175 (threshold varies by country):
- Ask for a VAT refund form (Tax Free form) at the shop
- Get it stamped at the airport before check-in
- Submit at the VAT refund desk at the airport or by mail

## Airport and Flight Tips

### Check-In
- Online check-in opens 24–48 hours before departure — do it early for better seat selection
- Arrive at the airport: 3 hours before international flights, 2 hours for domestic
- Have your passport and boarding pass accessible (digital or printed)

### Baggage
- Liquid rule: containers max 100ml, all in one clear 1L zip-lock bag (carry-on)
- Laptop and large electronics must be removed from bag at security
- Pack valuables and medications in carry-on (never in checked baggage)
- Take a photo of your checked luggage before departure for insurance claims

### Connecting Flights
- Minimum connection time: 1.5 hours (same terminal), 2.5 hours (different terminals or re-check)
- SunPath includes connection protection — if you miss a connection due to a delay on the first leg, we rebook you at no cost
- If the first flight is delayed 3+ hours, you may be entitled to compensation under EU261 (EU flights) or US DOT rules (US domestic)
